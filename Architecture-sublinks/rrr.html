Axonometric Site Overview

Social Housing Redevelopment


Barkly Street Social Housing is a 12 story building that currently houses 216 different tenants from lower socio-economic backgrounds. Targeted at housing a mainly older demographic with a wide array of different needs, the building currently offers two elevators that service the whole building, laundry facilities located on each floor, a garbage room that connects to a garbage chute that runs throughout the whole builing and emergency buzzers equipped in every unit to alert neighbours of any ongoing emergencies.

Currently, the building is planned to be demolished due to the building not meeting modern living standard combined with its outdated ammenities and thermal performance and is intended to be replaced with modern facilities in its place.

Our proposal looks to challenge this approach through instead suggesting a Retrofit and Infil approach to revitalising the towers in order to preserve pre existing communities within the building and to reduce the potential displacement of residents during construction of the new buildings.

Strategies that could be implemented within the building to enhance both its functional use and its energy performance in lieu of a complete demolition include the installation of a new facade element to attatch onto the pre-existing concrete structure to increase space within the apartments, increasing the livibility within the building as well as reducing fuel poverty through upgrading thermal insulation within the building as well as installing triple glazed windows and upgraded insulation to decrease operation costs of the building which could cut costs for the residents and offset the cost of these proposed additions.

Other strategies to improve the sense of safety and community within the building would be to promote the idea of ‘micro communities’ through improving elevators within the structure to lock out residents to particular floors and improving lines of sight within public areas such as corridors and ammenity rooms such as the laundry room and garbage areas to encourage residents to look out for each other. Nooks and crannies within the building will also look to be repurposed with a particular activity to improve safety and the ground floor will also be revitalised to allow a communal place to meet other residents without having to open personal units to facilitate this type of communication.
